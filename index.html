Mainpat

Mainpat is a hill station and small village in the Surguja district in the northern part of the state of Chhattisgarh, India. It lies about 55 kilometres (34 mi) by road from Ambikapur. This hill station is 50 km south of divisional headquarter of Ambikapur, 160 km northeast of Korba, and 360 km northeast of state capital Raipur.

INFORMATION

Mainpat is a quaint hill station in Chhattisgarh that is located in the Surguja district. Often referred to as Shimla of Chhattisgarh or even Mini Tibet, this small village has been inhabited by a large number of Tibetan refugees. Adding to the cultural diversity, travelers here get a chance to experience a unique blend along with the virgin beauty of the landscape that has remained undisturbed and unspoilt due to less tourist influx. Offering a breathtaking view of gorgeous valleys, enchanting waterfalls and gurgling rivulets, Mainpat is a must visit place for people who need a heavy dose of natureâ€™s bliss.

Best Time To Visit Mainpat

The monsoon and winter months are the best time to visit Mainpat as the temperature remains comfortable. So, you can plan your Mainpat Trip any time between July to August and October to December which are considered as the best months to discover this less explored region of Chhattisgarh.

History & Culture Of Mainpat

In the years 1962 to 1963, about 1400 Tibetans migrated to India and 3000 acres of land in Mainpat hill was allotted to these refugees by the Government of India. Since then, this erstwhile home to Yadavs and tribal communities such as Manjhi, Manjhwar, Kanwar, Pahadi Korwa became an abode for these immigrants. Today Mainpat boasts a large population of Buddhists and features many significant monasteries that are visited by a large number of Buddhists from all over the world.

Tiger Point

Located at a distance of 7 km from the center of Mainpat is a popular attraction called Tiger Point. Basically this spot has a beautiful waterfall that originates from Mahadev Muda River; this waterfall is surrounded by lush green and dense forests that make the place even more scenic. It is said that many people have spotted tigers in that area and hence the name came to be Tiger Point. Falling from a height of 60 m, the water falling from the waterfall makes a pool where several tourists sit and spend a relaxing day. This is one of the top attractions in Mainpat.

Machali Point

Machali point, also known as Fish Point is a spot that has a great view of a scenic waterfall. The main attraction of this place is that you can see cute little colorful fish in the stream where the water gets collected from the waterfall. This place is almost 17 km from the main hill station and it is nestled amidst green forests and waterfalls. This point has panoramic views of hills along with waterfalls and is a great place to visit while in Mainpat. If you are an avid angler, fish point is something that you will enjoy. Join locals in the sport of fishing and take back your catch for making dinner later.

Bouncing Land

Looking for something more fun in Mainpat? Visit the Bouncing land in this hill station, also known as Jaljali locally. The main attraction here is literally the land that bounces; at this spot, the landmass is such that if you jump on it, it acts like a trampoline along with giving out vibrations. The vibrations that are produced is a natural phenomenon and is a wonder of nature. When one jumps, the entire area shakes, making it quite fun. This is a great place to visit, especially if you have children; they are bound to enjoy this attraction.

Tatapani

Mainpat has a lot of natural resources, one of them being Tatapani, a natural hot water spring. This attraction is distanced at 12 km from the center of Mainpat and it has quite a history. Mythology says that during the era of Ramayan, Shri Ram during game play accidentally spilt hot oil from a bowl straight the ground. It is said that when these oil droplets fell to the ground, they made a hole so deep that hot water started to seep out of those holes. People living in Mainpat consider Tatapani to be sacred and believe that people taking a Bath in this reservoir can get rid of their diseases and sins. You can see a lot of tourists in this place during the festival of Makar Sankranti.
